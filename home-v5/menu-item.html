From the kitchen

Three ingredients, nowhere to hide.

A Margherita is a confession. There's no sauce thick enough to mask a flat dough, no cheese loud enough to forgive a sour tomato. When we built this kitchen, the first thing we agreed on was simple — if we couldn't make this one pizza honest, we had no business making any of the others.

The flour is a soft Tipo "00" from Caputo, hydrated to 65% and rested cold for three nights. The mozzarella is broken by hand the morning of service — never grated, never the night before. Basil goes on after the oven, while the crust is still loud. That's the only secret.

— Chef Marco Vitelli
